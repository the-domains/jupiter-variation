Various Artists - Decades of Destruction (Hammer 1986 - 2016)

To be honest, nowadays most of the magazine cover-mount CDs are pure shit (and the recent Kerrang tribute to Iron Maiden is a fine example of this) so to really be able to deliver an interesting result (which will eventually boost the sales in a slowly dying market) someone has to come up with something really unique, possibly by incorporating a few of the following things :

  • An original and (most important) interesting concept
  • A-Class participants
  • …who will be able to bring their own ideas in the concept and not just try to replay great songs which they were unable to write in the first place themselves
  • A decent budget for the recording, production and overall packaging

So taking the anniversary CD for the 30 first years of Hammer (the magazine that was once known as English Metal Hammer - the word “Metal” went away from the title during the grunge years when anything that had to do with it was not so…cool anymore) we have :

  • Concept : Has been done before and (mostly) is not representative of the 30 years period (1986-2016) that Hammer is celebrating
  • Participants : Korn, David Townsend, Sabbaton, Asking Alexandria, Katatonia amongst others…not bad…not bad at all…
  • Execution : Mostly great (will go into more details about that next)
  • Production and overall packaging : Great but could use a better cover though (3 skulls in a black background - oh how original)

Going into the track-list one by one :

  1. Korn - We Care a lot (Faith no More) : Probably the highlight of the compilation. Korn prove why they are such a huge band by increasing the dynamic of an established classic. Can I dare to say that I prefer this version over Faith no More’s?
  2. Asking Alexandria - Duality (Slipknot) : Nothing special or different than the original but also not bad. Decent.
  3. David Townsend - Fast as a Shark (Accept) : Another highlight of the album. Staying close to the original, Townsend delivers a top class performance.
  4. New Years Day - Mobscene (Marilyn Manson) : The bet with the female vocals, which give a more melodic (yet powerful) taste to the song, is won.
  5. Sabbaton - Kingdom Come (Manowar) : Even though I like Sabbaton much, I do not like their delivery here. Probably it has to do that with the fact that they chose one of the weakest Manowar tracks.
  6. Katatonia - Night Comes Down (Judas Priest) : Young bands take notes. This is how you take another band’s song and make it your own. Another highlight.
  7. Cane Hill - We Die Young (Alice in Chains) : Mediocre. Misses the dark feeling of the original.
  8. Hacktivist - Break Stuff (Limp Bizkit) : The original song is an absolute turd so the chances to deliver something decent here were reduced to minimum in advance.
  9. A. Wilson - Sleep Now in the Fire (Rage Against the Machine) : Staying close to the original a fresh breath is given. I like it much…very much…
  10. The Qemists - Blind (Korn) : Another great delivery…And by the way Korn is the only band who performs a cover and is being covered in the same time in this compilation.
  11. Employed to Serve - Memphis Will Be Laid to Waste (Norma Jean) : One more success. Heavy as fuck, takes no prisoners and makes real justice to the original.
  12. Counting Days - Disciple (Slayer) : A deathcore interpretation of Slayer ? No thanks…
  13. Stoneghost - I Disappear (Metallica) : Of all the Metallica songs out there, why this one? Weak performance, especially on the guitars…
  14. Mammoth Weed Wizard Bastard - Scum (Napalm Death) : Genius ! Just listen to what they did by taking the riff of Scum and transforming it into a sludge standard. Cool band name also.
  15. No Consequence - The Pot (Tool) : Nothing special but also not bad. Probably a different choice of a cover song could have helped them more.

Concluding, this is one of the most successful tribute compilations that has ever come out with a magazine as it gives a new perspective to a lot of classic songs. If I could point only one fault, that would be the fact that the chosen songs do not equally cover the 30 years period between 1986-2016. And by the way…where is Iron Maiden ?

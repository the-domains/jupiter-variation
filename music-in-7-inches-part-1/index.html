Music in 7 inches, part 1

Hissing - Cairn/Husk (2016)

Hissing come from Seattle and they play Grim, Blackened Death Metal with absolutely no sign of melody or groove. The 7 inch comes out from Southern Lord in a wonderful clear vinyl and is worthy every dime of the 6,66 USD that it costs. Hissing’s only other release is a self titled 4-track cassette E.P.

facebook.com/hissingseattle

hissingseattle.bandcamp.com

Trivia : Guitarist Joe O' Maley is the youngest brother of Sunn O)))/Burning Witch/Khanate/KTL guitarist Stephen O’Malley .

Hellhammer - Blood Insanity (2016)

Planned to be initially released in 1984 through Tom Gabriel’s Fischer’s own Prowling Death Records as a NWOBHM style 2-song promo, this 7 inch had to be finally postponed due to financial difficulties. Now in 2016 the abandoned (but apparently not forgotten project) is resurrected and the 7 inch is finally released via Century Media Records. Side A contains Blood Insanity with Maniac being on the b-side. The recorded material is taken from the Triumph of Death rehearsals so is intended mainly for the Hellhammer completists. For the starters, the (relatively) more accessible Apocalyptic Raids would be recommended.

Uncle Acid and the Deadbeats - Pusher Man (2016)

I start with the b-side of this single which is none other than the fantastic cover version of Iron Maiden’s Remember Tomorrow. Included originally in the Maiden Heaven 2 Iron Maiden tribute cd which was published with Kerrang! magazine, is one fine example of how a cover version can be original and respectful at the same time. With the exception of Uncle Acid and the Deadbeats and Anaal Nathrakh (they covered Powerslave) the rest of the tribute cd was absolute shit and by knowing that Anaal Nathrakh’s new album will include Powerslave as a bonus, I am happy because I can finally safely convert the Kerrang! cd into a beer mat.

Side A contains Pusher Man which comes from last year’s exceptional The Night Creeper album. The single comes in a beautiful purple vinyl and I would call it with one word as essential.

Goat - It’s Time For Fun (2015)

Released in August of 2015, this is another addition to the series of Goat 7 inch singles. Side A It’s Time for Fun starts with a bass line that brings to mind White Stripes’ Seven Nation Army, however all resemblances stop there as the song continues with a fantastic guitar riff which unless you are braindead will make you start dancing in your chair. B-Side Relax is an instrumental jam, driven by a standard Jazz rhythm and psychedelic keyboards. Good, but nothing special for a band of their level.

Goat - I Sing In Silence (2016)

A pre taste of 2016’s upcoming album, contains I Sing In Silence in Side A which carries all the afro-oriental influences that made Goat well known. This is a promising 3-minute sample from the new record but apparently left me wanting for more. B-Side The Snake of Addis Ababa, is a fantastic instrumental. Build upon a strong (however basic) bass line, the guitar creates an ecstatic, almost shamanic result. If someone wants to create a soundtrack for an orgy then he better includes this song in. I am really looking forward for the upcoming Requiem album.

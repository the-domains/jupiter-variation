Marriages - Salome (2015)

Truth is that this is a late discovery since Salome was released back in 2015 but nevertheless, better late than never. Marriages were formed by Emma Ruth Rundle and Greg Burns after Red Sparrows went on hiatus in 2012. With the addition of Dave Clifford in drums they released an E.P. (Kitsune 2012) and toured during 2013 with other Sargent House stars like Chelsea Wolfe, Russian Circles and Deafheaven. In 2014, Burns was replaced by Andrew Clinco and with their finalised line up, Marriages recorded and released Salome in 2015.

Music-wise Salome is not a big departure from Red Sparrows sound however what really makes the difference is Emma Ruth Rundle’s voice which contributes to the general dark and melancholic feeling that is overall present during the whole duration of the record. Salome is not one of those genre defining records. But…it is one of those beautiful and noble records who will instantly become your personal favourites and accompany you in certain moments of your life. It worked for me one autumn night during a walk along the Rhine river....

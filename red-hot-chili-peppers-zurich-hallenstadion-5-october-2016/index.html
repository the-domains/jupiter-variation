Red Hot Chili Peppers - Zurich Hallenstadion, 5 October 2016

Halfway through the concert I overheard a girl who was standing next to me saying to her boyfriend that this is a strange gig for her. Well for sure it was a strange gig, especially for the ones who got to know RHCP during the Californication era or the ones who mention bands like RHCP when they claim to their friends that they listen to Alternative Rock (sic). Without wanting to sound like an elitist (or at least too much), I felt that this concert was for the ones who really love RHCP for being the pioneers who pushed the envelope with albums like Mother’s Milk or for being the geniuses who delivered albums intelligent in their simplicity like Blood Sugar Sex Magic…this is what I was thinking while seeing the puzzled faces of many of the spectators during songs like Nobody Weird Like Me (who was delivered with incredible ferocity) or the questioning silence during the improvisational jams that were played between nearly each song…this puzzlement was becoming much more apparent every time RHCP were returning to their radio standards…at these moments the audience was suddenly reacting like a volcano erupting…I have to say that RHCP manipulated the audience wisely…they left out many concert standards (Under the Bridge for example) and this made the audience anticipate and react like crazy each time (finally) a hit-single was (flawlessly) played between each noisy, improvisational jam…well played trick, really well played…
